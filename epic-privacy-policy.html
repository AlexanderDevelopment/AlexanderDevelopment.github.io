Epic Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 May 2026

This privacy policy describes data processing related to Epic Games account services, Epic Online Services, and Epic Games Store features used by ZweiBier Indie games or applications.

1. Controller

The controller responsible for this Epic-related application data is:

Alex / ZweiBier Indie
Germany
Email: alex@zweibier-indie.de

2. Epic services covered by this policy

This policy applies when a ZweiBier Indie game or application uses Epic account sign-in, Epic Online Services, Epic Games Store features, cross-play related services, multiplayer sessions, invites, friends features, achievements, or similar Epic-provided functionality.

3. Data we may receive or process

Depending on the Epic services enabled for a specific game or application, we may process the following categories of data:

  • Epic account identifiers, such as an Epic Account ID or Product User ID.
  • Public Epic profile information needed for gameplay features, such as display name.
  • Authentication, entitlement, ownership, session, lobby, invite, matchmaking, or achievement information required to provide game functionality.
  • Technical information such as platform, app version, device and network diagnostics, crash information, and logs needed for security, debugging, support, and abuse prevention.
  • Messages or support information you choose to send to us.

4. Why the data is used

Epic-related data is used only to operate and improve the game or application, verify account access and entitlements, enable multiplayer and social features, provide support, protect the service from abuse, fix bugs, and comply with legal or platform obligations.

5. Data shared with Epic

Epic Games may process data independently when you use Epic accounts, Epic Online Services, or Epic Games Store features. Epic's own processing is governed by Epic's privacy policy and account terms. We do not sell Epic user data and do not use Epic user data for advertising profiling.

6. Data shared with other parties

We may share limited data with service providers only when needed to operate the game, provide support, host infrastructure, diagnose crashes, maintain security, or comply with legal duties. Service providers are expected to process data only for the requested service and under appropriate confidentiality and security obligations.

7. Legal basis

Where applicable, processing is based on performance of a contract when data is needed to provide the game or requested online features, legitimate interests in operating and securing the service, consent where a feature requires it, and compliance with legal obligations where required.

8. Data retention

Epic-related data is retained only as long as needed for the purposes described above, including gameplay operation, support, security, debugging, legal compliance, and dispute handling. Retention periods may vary depending on the specific game, platform feature, log type, and legal obligation.

9. Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or objection to processing of your personal data. You may also have the right to lodge a complaint with a competent data protection authority. For Epic account data controlled by Epic, please use Epic's account and privacy tools.

10. Children's privacy

ZweiBier Indie games and applications are not intended to knowingly collect personal data from children without the required legal basis, parental consent, or platform controls. If you believe a child has provided personal data directly to us, contact us so we can review and remove it where required.

11. Contact

For privacy questions, GDPR requests, deletion requests, correction requests, objection requests, portability requests, or other data protection inquiries related to ZweiBier Indie processing, contact: alex@zweibier-indie.de.

12. Changes

This policy may be updated if Epic services, game features, data processing practices, service providers, or legal requirements change.
